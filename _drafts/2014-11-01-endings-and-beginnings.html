 I observe my mother gently have her mind depart her body.
She tells me this is the end is near
Though she cannot remember the name of the day
Or why she needs the patch I place
On her while pealing off yesterdays
The "memory loss patch"
And we laugh.

Every. Single. Morning.

And menopause arrives.

I still see her wisdom shine through.
I trust and believe her
Most especially when I'm exhausted from
Giving care.

We've made a deal
If she is having a freatting moment or is fixated
She is to ask me
So I can clear the air
Reassurance works
when remembered as an option

Her therapist tells me to
cal myself her outside
hard-drive
which works when
remembered as an option

I resort to thinking
As she did
when
I was the child.
Before a test I woke
in the middle of the night
Starlted out of sleep realzing
you can only do
the best you can do

In Doctor's offices
We discuss the most
private of matters
Then we giggle.
We are professionals now
at paperwork and giggling.

And I weep.
Tears fall
more gently than before
based on gratittude

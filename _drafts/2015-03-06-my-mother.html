I intend to use this space to explain and explore some of how I feel, some of how she does and how we cope with all of it.  I have full permission, in the hopes that others may learn, relate and feel better in their own experiences.

____________________________________

Doctor's visit 101

My nephew and his friend were visiting us from China the morning that my very own mother did not recognize me.  It was terrifying on layers I'm unable to put into words as of yet.  As mentioned above, we'd not spent a great deal of time together in over 12 years and I missed her.

The morning she didn't know who I was, taught me LOUD AND CLEAR that I had no idea what actual "missing" was.

I insisted that we do some further exploration into what may be happening, and while that was a good plan, it also meant that we would learn the eventual truth.  And I wondered, often, whether or not it was worth the trek to specialists and hospitals 5 hours from her comfort zone - from home, to uncover what was truly happening.

We knew what was happening, but no many of us, in the family were willing to deal with any of it; denial became a river we'd had loved to canoe down despite warnings.

Mind you,  my timing was spot on.


so I took her to a couple of local docs she'd been using for years and then I realized, it was time to break out the big guns.  So my aunt, a pal and Mom drove to Scott and white in Temple, Texas.  I remember as they drove off feeling as though I should go, but someone had to take care of the dog.

On the way home, after not much news on the doctor front,a tire blew out on a bridge with not much of a side place to pull off upon and my mom's pal saved their lives.

Those were the good old days.

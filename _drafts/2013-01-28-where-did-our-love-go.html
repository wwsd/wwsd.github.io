This is a bigger conversation that just "let gay people marry".You know that as much as I do - this is a civil rights discussion.  Period. 

Our nation has had this conversation time and time again.  Remember that era where women had to fight to vote?  Or that Civil War (by the way, I've been meaning to ask - what is civil about war?) That whole time when we let anyone be able to urinate in any restroom or sit anywhere on a bus.

OH, that reminds me, I've got a couple of questions for you before you even think you can marry.  1) so, do you do it doggie style?  Because, we can't have people that love each other doing that kind of sexual act.  It would scar our nation.  oh and 2) if you give your hubby a  blow job, do you swallow?  As anation we frown on any kind of potential sperm loss.  3) do you have sexual fantasies?  Because well, that' just plainly inappropriate because I said so, that's why.

I don't think that we have any idea how far and deep our words  go.

A friend told me that someone had been over the top rude to her today.  I shared a story of a customer I'd had years ago that went 'postal rude' on me until I simply asked him why he felt the need to be so rude to me.  You could have heard a pin drop.  He didn't have any bit of a clue that he was being rude.  So, I'm going to pretend that you haven't a clue that you are being rude to me by telling me I can't marry a woman, OK?  Do you see now that you are being rude? 

this battle we are having these days feels to me as though society is turning it's back on love.  Think about it.

Did I miss something here?  I thought this nation was one of opportunity.  Of hope. 

I understand fear of change.  It's the only thing I can come up with that actually makes a tinge of sense around this fight against love.

I"m fairly certain that even Tomas Jefferson would be ashamed of this great nation's behavior about love.

And if it's NOT OK for a woman to marry a woman - why is it OK for a man to have more than one wife?  I can't find a difference other than testoserone.

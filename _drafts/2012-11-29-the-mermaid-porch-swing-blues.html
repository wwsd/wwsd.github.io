--- layout: post title: The Mermaid Porch Swing Blues date: '2012-11-29T14:59:00.004-08:00' author: Jennifer Rowan tags: modified_time: '2012-11-29T14:59:47.277-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-741389610085892054.post-8464629006522446809 blogger_orig_url: http://splashofamermaid.blogspot.com/2012/11/the-mermaid-porch-swing-blues.html --- It's true.  Even mermaids with porch swings get the blues.  Thank my lucky stars (sun, moons and planets, too) that usually music, a good book and memories can cure whatever might be feeling like an ailment.  

I recently had the incredible experience to meet a dear friend of mine from New Orleans in Chicago.  He had a speaker he wanted to see and it was closer to where I was living at the time and I flew in to meet him.  We had a fabulous adventure - from Dunkin Donuts to wandering, to allowing time for rest that we both dearly needed.  I wrote - he'd lunch with a pal, and so on.  It's one of those major gifts from the universe to find someone who is such a lovely travel companion.  It was a divine gift during a period that felt like had not end in sight - I am nearly swallowed by gratitude just thinking of that trip. 

Music is a vital part of my life.  One afternoon, I meandered back to the hotel from seeing a play, filled with joy and at each corner on the Lake Michigan there was someone playing an instrument into the street going away from the lake - it echoed lovlieness to me.  Someone to Watch Over Me, >>>>> and I couldn't tell you what else other than it spoke to my spirit.  That was lots of happiness brewing in the people walking home or whereever.  The music was very soundtrackish and rounded out that particular stroll for me.

Our last night there, we saw the speaker (that's a WHOLE other post of lovely) had dinner with a friend we'd connected with then realzied we'd not yet had a full taste of the Chicago Blues.  After summoning some energy from a couple of gin and tonics, we made our way to the subway to head to a club my TC had been to before. 

We were heading out of town, so actually had to go down under to get the right line - and upon our descent (with an acapella serinade greeting us) I saw a daisy in full bloom growing from a tiny crevice in the subway wall.  Suddenly the adventures of the day and the adventures of the night were disceted and I found a new energy - I was ready.

Well, or so I thought.  We got to the club - in which I might add, I felt the history in a delightfully eerrie manner - sort of like when I had to sit down on the steps of the WEstminster abby as an 18 year old visiting England - I could feel the history in such a beatiful, deep way. 

Daisy growing out of the crack in the subway wall.

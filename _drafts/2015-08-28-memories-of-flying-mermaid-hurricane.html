--- layout: post title: Memories of a Flying Mermaid - Hurricane Katrina Rescue 10 Years On... date: '2015-08-28T17:51:00.000-07:00' author: Jennifer Rowan tags: modified_time: '2015-08-28T17:51:22.209-07:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-741389610085892054.post-4493575376608899912 blogger_orig_url: http://splashofamermaid.blogspot.com/2015/08/memories-of-flying-mermaid-hurricane.html --- AS you move through your day today, it might be nice if you'd take a moment to thank a person you know who works with animals in a shelter environment.   It's 10 years since Hurricane Katrina wrecked chaos across an entire region.

This particular mermaid would prefer to be  in or next to the ocean, or in the air soaring through the skies with a planeload of animals on their way to new families and safety.

PICTURE

I was involved with 923 directly affected hurricane Katrina animals, hundreds of volunteers, 9 airlifts, and thousands of other animals helped in the aftermath.

The stories are endless; each day was a novel.

People with philosophical differences showed up everywhere and made indelible, life-saving things happen.

Many times I was asked why I wasn't helping the people.  My response was simple - I AM.  I'm helping the animals that are family members to so many.

Many times I was asked "may we see an Katrina animal - we want to rescue one" .  About three interviews with the press in, I saw a trend and began to see the huge picture.  Every. Single.  Homeless. Pet.  Was. A victim of the storm, that left in my estimate upwards of 50,000 animals in distress.

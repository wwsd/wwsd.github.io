 There are several different takes on this in my mind.


As an animal care disaster relief specialist, thousands of animals are alive because of connections I made on the internet.

That's a bold, yet true, statement for me to make - as it was never about who or when or where, it was about the life of the animal and trust.

I didn't meet all of the people I sent animals to;  I didn't have the resources to tour  every home or ranch - but I relied on the fact that I could find references, photos and in many cases, honesty about the truth of the situation without having to travel myself from sea to shining sea.  I involved my instincts when things felt "not so perfect" and I made a life-long list of connections that will always be there for the animals AND me, because of that.  Thank you, internet.

Changing a life starts with wanting life to change.  

That's not my first experience with people I had "only" met on the internet. In 2001, I was in an online support group for a life experience I'd had that I was attempting to overcome.  I found a group of people that had been through similar situations.  I connected with a group of "sisters".  We have all conquered and overcome that situation, for the most part, thanks to US.  And yes, we've met in person so many times - there is no chance that we were not supposed to.

Support is support is support. 

Nearly a year ago, I moved to live with my mother, thinking I was the one that needed help, when we discovered (uncovered?) as a family that she has mixed dementia and that her living alone was no longer an option.  I have researched and discussed all of this with so many people on the internet that I am able to present options to her physicians about what I'm seeing happen that can help them make sense of it all.  I've connected with some who are either in or were in similar situations and I am more prepared than ever to take care of her because of these contacts.  I am more confident and compassionate in my care-giving, which in turn allows my mother to feel just the same towards me and others around that love her as much as I.

Big love is not the easiest thing to find.

A few years back, I read a book that literally set my life on a different path.  I wrote the author and she and I became friends; meanwhile I connected with many others that also felt the same sort of greatness that her words allowed.  I bow in grace to these friends - they are not false - some have sent me oils, other funds when I was at the end of my rope, others prayers and always love.  I know not what I would do without this inspirational crew of big love and I only hope I return half as much in gratitude and love.

Reunions

When I first discovered Facebook, I was excited, yet wary.  That is, until the love of my life and I re-connected after 20 years. I've since found family I didn't even realize I had; connected with dear, long-time friends and found people I didn't even know I'd missed.

What I know is it's not always the people you have coffee with each day that help you (or the animals) through the toughest of times.  The above article is inspirational in that point.  It shows the magnitude of connection - of being one with others.

In gratitude, Lisa.
